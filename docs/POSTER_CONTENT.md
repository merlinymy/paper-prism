# ARC Research Poster вҖ” Complete Content & Layout Guide

> Everything you need to create your poster. Copy the text directly, follow the layout, take the screenshots described.

---

## Poster Specs

- **Size**: 48" wide x 36" tall (landscape) вҖ” standard academic poster, most Makerspaces support this
- **Tool**: Use **Canva** (free, has poster templates), Google Slides (set custom size to 48x36 inches), or PowerPoint
- **Font sizes**: Title = 72-96pt, Section headers = 36-44pt, Body text = 24-28pt, Captions = 18-20pt
- **Rule of thumb**: If you can't read it from 4 feet away, the text is too small

### SECTION 1: The Problem (Left Column, Top)

**Header**: The Problem

**Body text** (keep it short вҖ” 4-5 bullet points max):

```
Researchers need answers grounded in THEIR papers вҖ” not generic LLM responses.

Current tools fall short:

  вҖў ChatGPT/Claude hallucinate citations and have no access 
    to your private paper library

  вҖў Existing open-source RAG systems use largely uniform 
    retrieval вҖ” they don't adapt chunk types, result counts,
    section filters, or diversity limits per query type

  вҖў A question about a specific IC50 value needs precise, 
    small chunks from a data table

  вҖў A question comparing methods across 20 papers needs broad 
    retrieval from many abstracts

  вҖў One retrieval strategy cannot serve both
```

**Callout box** (highlighted background):
```
Key Insight: Different questions need fundamentally 
different retrieval strategies. An adaptive pipeline
with quality feedback loop wins 13 of 15 queries
against raw Claude on blind, reference-grounded evaluation.
```

## Common Poster Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Too much text** вҖ” If a section has more than 6-7 lines, cut it. The poster supplements your verbal explanation; it doesn't replace it.
2. **Tiny fonts** вҖ” Nothing below 20pt. If it doesn't fit, cut words, not font size.
3. **No visual hierarchy** вҖ” The pipeline diagram and the query-adaptive table should be the two biggest visual elements. Someone walking by should see those first.
4. **Walls of bullets** вҖ” Use diagrams, tables, and screenshots instead of bullet lists wherever possible.
5. **Too many colors** вҖ” Stick to 3-4 colors max plus grays.
6. **No "try it" call-to-action** вҖ” The laptop demo is your strongest selling point. Make the poster actively direct people to it.
